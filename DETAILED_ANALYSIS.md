## Key Insights from Analysis

### ✓ What Makes a HIGH-Quality Lead

1. **Confirmed building type** (Ärztehaus, not "health center" or mixed-use)
2. **Ground floor available** (non-negotiable)
3. **Size in target range** (250–450 sqm, 400 sqm is sweet spot)
4. **Good accessibility** (transit, parking, wheelchair)
5. **Early stage** (concept/planning/approval = time to negotiate)
6. **High confidence** (95%+ = official sources)

**Real Example**: Leipzig-Gohlis ✓ all 6 factors → 98% confidence, 5/5 suitability → HIGH lead

### ⚠️ What Makes a MEDIUM-Quality Lead

- Has most factors but missing one (e.g., multi-level, peripheral location)
- Good confidence (80–92%)
- Needs more research or negotiation
- Still worth pursuing, but lower priority

**Real Example**: Dresden Altstadt ✓ most factors, but multi-level and slightly lower confidence → 85% confidence, 4/5 suitability → MEDIUM

### ❌ What Makes a LOW-Quality Lead

1. **Missing ground floor** (dealbreaker)
2. **Too small or too large** (<250 sqm or >500 sqm mixed-use)
3. **Late stage** (construction or marketing without contact)
4. **Uncertain building type** (generic "medical facility")
5. **Poor location** (peripheral, low foot traffic)
6. **Low confidence** (<80%, many unknowns)

**Real Examples**:
- Magdeburg: No ground floor → LOW
- Friedrichshain: Construction phase → too late → LOW
- Neustadt: Mixed-use complexity → LOW

## Scoring Logic Summary

**Suitability Score (1–5)**:
```
5/5 = Ground floor + ideal size + excellent location + accessible
4/5 = Ground floor + good size + good location (may have one minor issue)
3/5 = Ground floor + acceptable size OR excellent location but one issue
2/5 = Ground floor + small size OR non-ground floor OR poor location
1/5 = Multiple major issues (too small, non-accessible, wrong building type)
```

**Lead Quality**:
```
HIGH = Suitability 4–5 + Confidence 90%+ + Stage concept/planning/approval
MEDIUM = Suitability 3–4 + Confidence 80%+ + Manageable issues
LOW = Suitability 1–2 OR Confidence <75% OR Critical issues (no ground floor, construction)
```

**Confidence**:
```
95%+ = Official news sources, confirmed details
85–94% = Reliable sources, mostly confirmed
75–84% = Web sources, some details estimated
<75% = Uncertain, requires verification
```
